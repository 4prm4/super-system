Johnny Jash

Johnny Jash is a singer-songwriter born in Mexico. Born in Chicago,he was born March 21, 1973 and worked as a producer for two years prior to graduating from the University of Illinois at Chicago. In the mid-'90s, after his final release, he wrote two songs. He then returned to Los Angeles for the first time in over thirty years, where he recorded six new album singles. Following his release, he re-envisioned the '70s in the form of Bambas. With the exception of the live sets that occurred in early '70s Los Angeles, Jash's work with these new acts was the same. His work is sometimes referred to as "Vinyl" sound as he sang about the '80s, even though the band featured Bambas' songs on most of the songs. However, he had to wait until his first recording after he was diagnosed with Hodgkin's lymphoma to work on his next record. This left him without his favorite songs for quite sometime until the song "Don't Give 'Em Up" came out. "Don't Give 'Em Up" is his favorite song. It was one of the first albums that Jash wrote for Pink Floyd, being inspired by their previous album "You Don't Know Us" with Jeff Lynne. The song was written for "Don't Give Me Up" which debuted at Number 2 on the Billboard Hot 100 with a 1,531,539 lead single.

Molly Moonbeam

Molly Moonbeam is a Jamacian singer-songwriter born in 1976. Her music has reached a wide audience with her debut album, The Girl On The Train, released on April 28, 1986. Her vocals live in her own studio and as a result have been played on the popular album, The One With The Dummy. She has been performing professionally for over 25 years, including stints at many major labels including MTV, New York Times, the Daily Mail, NPR, The Atlantic and National Enquirer in the United States and abroad. She is most recently known for her debut record, One Last Time .. The song was written for "Don't Give Me Up" which debuted at Number 2 on the Billboard Hot 100 with a 1,531,539 lead single.
